Your Diabetes Story: Recognizing Your Experience

Understanding diabetes means recognizing both what you're feeling and why it's happening. This knowledge empowers you to make informed decisions.

What You Might Be Feeling
Constant Thirst & Urination

Feeling like you can't drink enough water, coupled with frequent trips to the bathroom.

Unexplained Fatigue

A deep tiredness that sleep doesn't seem to fix, as your body struggles to use fuel efficiently.

Blurred Vision

High blood sugar can affect the eye's lens, causing temporary vision changes.

Why This Is Happening
Insulin Resistance (Type 2)

Cells do not respond properly to insulin, making it harder for glucose to enter and be used for energy.

Insulin Deficiency (Type 1)

The pancreas produces little or no insulin due to an autoimmune process.

Persistently High Glucose

Without effective insulin action, glucose accumulates in the blood and drives symptoms.

Clinical Note

Diagnosis and medication adjustments must be made by your physician. Our role is to translate your diagnosis into a safe, personalized nutrition and lifestyle plan‚Äîand to coordinate with your care team.
