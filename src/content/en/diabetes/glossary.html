Glossary of Diabetes Terms

A

Autoimmune Condition
A condition in which the body's immune system mistakenly attacks its own tissues. In Type 1 Diabetes, it attacks the insulin-producing cells of the pancreas.

C

Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM)
A wearable device that tracks glucose levels continuously throughout the day and night, providing real-time data, trend information, and alerts.

D

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
A serious, life-threatening complication where the body produces high levels of ketones (a type of acid) due to a lack of insulin. It primarily affects people with Type 1 diabetes.

G

Glycemic Index (GI)
A ranking of carbohydrate-containing foods based on their effect on blood glucose levels. Low-GI foods cause a slower, lower rise in blood sugar.

H

HbA1c (or A1c)
A blood test that measures your average blood glucose levels over the past 2-3 months. It's a key indicator of long-term glucose control.
Hyperglycemia
High blood sugar. This occurs when the body has too little insulin or when the body can't use insulin properly.
Hypoglycemia
Low blood sugar. This is a potential side effect of some diabetes medications, particularly insulin, and can be dangerous if not treated promptly.

I

Insulin
A hormone made by the pancreas that allows your body to use sugar (glucose) from carbohydrates in the food that you eat for energy or to store glucose for future use.
Insulin Resistance
A condition in which the body's cells do not respond effectively to insulin. It is the hallmark of Type 2 Diabetes.

S

Self-Monitoring of Blood Glucose (SMBG)
The process of checking one's own blood sugar level using a glucose meter, typically via a finger-prick.

T

Time-in-Range (TIR)
A metric used with CGM data that shows the percentage of time a person's glucose levels are within their target range. A higher TIR is associated with a lower risk of complications.
